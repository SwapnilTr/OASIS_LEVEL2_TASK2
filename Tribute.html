Subrahmanyam Jaishankar

09 Jan 1955

Foriegn Secretary of India. In 2019, he was conferred with Padma Shri, India's fourth highest civilian honour.

Biography

Indian politician and diplomat S Jaishankar currently holds the position of minister in the Ministry of External Affairs. He was born in New Delhi on January 9, 1955. His primary education was completed at The Air Force School in New Delhi’s Subroto Park. He then earned a chemistry degree from St. Stephen’s College, University of Delhi. Additionally, he earned an MA in Political Science. Jaishankar earned his master’s degree and doctorate in international relations from Jawaharlal Nehru University as well. He is also a specialist in nuclear diplomacy. He prepared for UPSC and passed the Civil Service entrance exam after studying. In 1977, he joined the Indian Foreign Service, where, from 1979 to 1981, he was the second and third secretary for the Indian Mission in the Soviet Union in Moscow.He served as the Indian embassy’s first secretary in Washington, D.C., from 1985 to 1988. From 1988 to 1990, he was the first secretary and political adviser for the Indian Peacekeeping Force (IPKF) in Sri Lanka.
